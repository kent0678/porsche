Why I Love It

No Turbos.
No Compromises.

Most fast cars today rely on turbochargers and all-wheel drive to hit their numbers. The GT3 RS does it the hard way, it's a pure, screaming naturally aspirated flat-six, rear-wheel drive, and race car aerodynamics.

It's loud, physical, and raw. That's exactly why I love this car.

Naturally Aspirated

No turbos. A 4.0L flat-six revving all the way to 9,000 RPMs with an exhaust sound unlike anything else on earth.

Active Aero

The rear wing adjusts on the fly, DRS on straights, max downforce in corners. Straight from motorsport, but street legal.

Lightweight

Carbon fiber roof, hood, and front fenders. The Weissach Package saves an additional 40 lbs.

Racing DNA

The suspension, aero, and engine all come directly from the 911 RSR (an actual race car) that dominates GT Endurance racing worldwide.

History

Every Generation

The GT3 RS nameplate has been around since 2004. Here's how it evolved from an idea into the most extreme road car Porsche has ever built.

1999
996 Generation · The Beginning
The GT3 Badge is Born
Unveiled at the Geneva Motor Show in 1999, the 996 GT3 was the first car to wear the GT3 name. It was built purely as a homologation special — Porsche needed a road-legal version of the car they were racing in the FIA GT3 class. Powered by the legendary "Mezger" flat-six engine with roots in the Le Mans-winning 911 GT1 race car, it became the first road car to lap the Nürburgring in under 8 minutes. The RS variant followed in 2004 but was never officially sold in the US.

2006
997 Generation · The Purist's Choice
The Last True Analog GT3 RS
The 997 generation is what most enthusiasts think of when they picture the "perfect" GT3 RS. It returned to the classic 911 silhouette and brought the first Active Suspension Management (PASM) system. The 2007 RS variant added wider bodywork and a more focused setup. The series peaked in 2011 with the legendary GT3 RS 4.0 — a 500 hp, 4.0L limited edition (only 600 units made) widely considered one of the greatest driver's cars ever built.

2015
991 Generation · Modern Muscle
Bigger, Wider, More Downforce
The 991 GT3 RS was a major leap forward in size and downforce. The wider Turbo bodyshell gave it a muscular stance, and the front fender air vents became a signature visual detail. This was also the first GT3 RS to drop the manual gearbox entirely — only the 7-speed PDK dual-clutch was offered, which upset purists but allowed faster lap times. The 4.0L engine screamed to 8,800 RPM and generated 330 kg of downforce.

992 Generation · The Current Model
Active Aero. Race Car Suspension. 518 HP.
The current 992 GT3 RS is the most extreme road-going 911 ever made. Porsche spent over 250 hours in the wind tunnel to develop its active aerodynamics — the rear wing adjusts automatically for DRS on straights and maximum downforce in corners. The suspension is lifted directly from the 911 RSR race car. It generates a staggering 409 kg of downforce. In 2024, Porsche test driver Lars Kern lapped the Nürburgring in 6:59 — the first sub-7-minute lap by a road car with a combustion engine.

Built to conquer the Nürburgring

The Nürburgring Nordschleife is a 12.9-mile race circuit in Germany with 73 corners, elevation changes over 900 feet, and a reputation that has humbled every car manufacturer on earth. The GT3 RS has been setting records there since 1999 and its getting faster every generation.
